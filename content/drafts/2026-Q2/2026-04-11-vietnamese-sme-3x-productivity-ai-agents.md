## Introduction

Minh Logistics (name changed for confidentiality) is a mid-sized logistics company in Ho Chi Minh City with 45 employees. They handle freight forwarding, customs brokerage, and last-mile delivery for export companies across the Mekong Delta.

Like most Vietnamese SMEs, they faced a familiar problem: too much manual work, not enough people, and growing client demands.

This is the story of how they deployed AI agents — starting with invoice processing and customer support — and tripled their team's productivity in three months.

---

## The Challenge: Growth Without Headcount

### The Bottleneck

By Q3 2025, Minh Logistics was winning new contracts but struggling to deliver:

- **Invoice processing** took 3 full-time staff, 6 hours per day
- **Customer inquiries** were handled by 4 staff, with 40% of calls being "where is my shipment?" status checks
- **Data entry errors** caused 12% of shipments to have documentation issues, resulting in delays

The operations manager, Ms. Nguyen Thi Hien, estimated they were turning down 15% of potential new business because they didn't have the capacity to handle more.

### The Decision

In October 2025, after attending a workshop on AI for logistics, Hien proposed a pilot: deploy AI agents for the two most time-consuming tasks — invoice processing and customer support tracking.

She presented a simple calculation:

| Cost | Traditional Hiring | AI Agent |
|------|-------------------|----------|
| Monthly cost | ₫45M (salary + benefits) | ₫2.99M |
| Onboarding time | 2-3 months | 1 week |
| Available hours | 176 hours/month | 720 hours/month |
| Error rate | 5-8% | <2% |

The math was clear. The CEO approved a 90-day pilot.

## The Implementation

### Phase 1: Setup (Weeks 1-2)

**What they did:**
1. Connected AIEmployee to their existing TKT Invoice system
2. Uploaded 6 months of historical invoice data for the AI to learn from
3. Configured the customer support AI with their FAQ database and tracking webhook from their logistics system

**Challenges:**
- Initial setup required IT support for 2 days (their team had basic technical skills)
- Some invoice formats from older clients required custom templates

**Time invested:** Approximately 20 hours total across the team

### Phase 2: Training (Weeks 3-4)

**What they did:**
- Processed 500 invoices with human review of each output
- AI accuracy started at 82%, improved to 96% by week 4
- Customer support AI learned their 47 common inquiry types

**Key learning:**
> "The AI didn't just learn our processes — it started catching errors we didn't even know we had. By week 3, it flagged 3 invoices with incorrect HS codes that our team had missed for months." — Ms. Nguyen Thi Hien, Operations Manager

### Phase 3: Autonomous Operation (Weeks 5-12)

**What changed:**
- Invoice processing became 85% autonomous
- Customer support AI handled 70% of status inquiries without human intervention
- Staff time spent on manual data entry dropped from 6 hours/day to 45 minutes/day

## Lessons Learned

### What Worked Well

**1. Starting with the biggest time sink**
Invoice processing was consuming the most staff hours. Fixing that first gave the biggest immediate impact.

**2. Incremental autonomy**
They didn't try to automate everything at once. The AI started with 50% autonomy, moved to 85% by month 3.

**3. Dedicated human owner**
One operations staff member was designated as "AI trainer" — responsible for reviewing outputs and adjusting settings. This ownership made the difference.

**4. Treating AI as a team member**
Monthly reviews, feedback sessions, and performance metrics were tracked just like a human employee.

### What They Would Do Differently

**1. Start smaller**
They initially tried to handle 3 AI agent roles simultaneously. They recommend starting with one.

**2. Include the team earlier**
Some staff were nervous about AI at first. Involving them in training from day one would have reduced resistance.

**3. Document processes first**
The AI learned faster when they documented their workflows before implementation. They recommend doing this in week 1.
