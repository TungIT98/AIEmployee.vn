## 3. Language Style

### 3.1 Sentence Structure
- **Lead with value:** Start sentences with the benefit, not the feature
- **Keep it short:** Aim for 15–25 words per sentence
- **Use active voice:** "We built X" not "X was built"
- **One idea per sentence:** Avoid comma-chains longer than 3 items

### 3.2 Vocabulary Rules

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| AI employee, AI agent, virtual employee | Robot, chatbot, automated system |
| Your business, your team | The user, customers (in general messaging) |
| Scale, grow, expand | Boom, explode, skyrocket |
| Save time, reduce cost | Eliminate humans, replace staff |
| Smart automation | Magic, AI magic, revolutionary AI |
| Get started, try free | Buy now, purchase, sign up (casual) |

### 3.3 Vietnamese Voice (English Content)
- Translate concepts, not words
- Vietnamese culture values: long-term relationships, trust, modesty, community
- Avoid: direct "you/your" accusations, aggressive promises, comparison to competitors by name
- Use: inclusive "we/our", collaborative framing, Vietnamese cultural context in examples

## 5. Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- ✅ Speak to business outcomes (revenue, time saved, efficiency gains)
- ✅ Use specific, measurable claims when possible
- ✅ Show Vietnamese businesses and professionals in examples
- ✅ Reference real use cases (invoicing, customer support, data entry)
- ✅ Be confident — "We built this", "Our AI does X"
- ✅ Explain complex concepts in one simple sentence
- ✅ Use "your" — "Your team", "Your business", "Your growth"

### Don'ts
- ❌ Use hype language: "game-changing", "revolutionary", "disruptive"
- ❌ Promise unrealistic timelines or guaranteed ROI percentages
- ❌ Mention competitor names (positive or negative)
- ❌ Claim AI is "perfect" or "always accurate"
- ❌ Use fear-based marketing ("don't get left behind")
- ❌ Describe AI as replacing human jobs in marketing content
- ❌ Use slang, emojis in formal copy, or casual internet speak
- ❌ Make claims without Vietnamese context or local examples

## 6. Content Types & Patterns

### 6.1 Social Media Posts (LinkedIn/Facebook)

**Opening Pattern:** Hook + Value Proposition + CTA

```
[Stat/Fact about Vietnamese SMB challenges] → [How AIEmployee solves it] → [Try it / Learn more]

Example:
"Vietnamese businesses spend 40% of admin time on repetitive tasks.
AIEmployee handles it — invoice processing, data entry, scheduling — 24/7.
Ready to reclaim that time? → aiemployee.vn/demo"
```

### 6.2 Blog Articles

**Structure:** Problem → Solution → How It Works → Example → CTA

- H1: Outcome-focused (not feature-focused)
- Intro: Lead with the pain point Vietnamese businesses face
- Body: How AIEmployee addresses it, with Vietnamese examples
- Conclusion: Summarize benefit, soft CTA

### 6.3 Landing Page Copy

**Hero Section:**
- Headline: Clear outcome or transformation
- Subheadline: 1-2 sentences explaining what and why
- CTA: Action-oriented, low-friction ("Get Started Free", "See Demo")

**Feature Sections:**
- Icon/Heading: Benefit not feature name
- 2-3 sentence explanation: What it does + Why it matters
- Optional: One-line social proof or metric

### 6.4 Email Sequences

**Subject Line:** Specific outcome + Urgency (when appropriate)
- "Your AI employee is ready to handle [task]"
- "Reduce [cost] by [X%] — here's how"
- "Vietnamese businesses are scaling with AI — see how"

**Body:** 3-4 short paragraphs max, clear CTA in every email

## 8. Avoid Words & Phrases

| Avoid | Replace With |
|-------|-------------|
| Game-changer | Effective, impactful |
| Revolutionary | Innovative, advanced |
| Disruptive | Transformative |
| Magic / Magical | Smart, automated |
| Amazing / Incredible | Effective, powerful |
| Groundbreaking | New capability |
| "Don't miss out" | "Here's how to get started" |
| "Limited time" (without real deadline) | Specific offer with real timeline |
| AI will replace you | AI will help you |
| "Easy" (as a claim) | Show, don't say — give a specific example |

## 9. Grammar & Format Standards

- **Capitalization:** Title Case for headings, sentence case for body
- **Numbers:** Use numerals (5, not five) for stats and measurements; spell out for general references
- **Vietnamese terms:** Use original Vietnamese terms with English translation on first use
- **Dates:** Use ISO format (2026-04-09) in documentation; "April 9, 2026" in public content
- **Punctuation:** Oxford comma avoided in marketing copy; semicolons avoided entirely
- **Links:** Use descriptive anchor text, never "click here"
- **Lists:** Use bullet points with parallel structure (all noun phrases or all verb phrases)

## 10. Channel-Specific Guidelines

### LinkedIn
- Professional tone, B2B focus
- Use data points and specific outcomes
- End with a question or invite to discuss
- Image posts get 2x engagement — use screenshots, infographics, team photos

### Facebook
- More conversational than LinkedIn
- Can use slightly shorter sentences
- Use Vietnamese context in examples
- Community engagement: reply to comments within 2 hours

### Email Marketing
- Personalize subject line with recipient name or company
- Mobile-first formatting (60%+ opens on mobile)
- Plain text version available (no images as only content)
- Unsubscribe clearly visible

### Website / Landing Pages
- Primary language: English for global, Vietnamese for local landing pages
- F-pattern reading pattern: important info in top-left quadrant
- Trust signals visible: client logos, case studies, security badges

## 11. Compliance Notes

- No misleading claims about AI capabilities
- No comparison to named competitors
- No promises of "100% accuracy" or "perfect results"
- Privacy statements on all forms collecting data
- Clear disclosure when content is AI-generated (where required)
